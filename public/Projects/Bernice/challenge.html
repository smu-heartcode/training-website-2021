What are some challenges they face?


Outdoor difficulties

Blind people face lots of difficulties while being outside due to their inabilities to see.
For example,
They are unable to avoid floating obstacles such as branches that are not in the range that the cane can reached
They have difficulties in using tactile paving that is blocked by obstacles
They are also unable to read signs and bus numbers

Tactile paving
Use to convey messages,
such as hazard warnings,
directional guiding, to the blind.

 Block tactile path

Finding a job

Being blind decreases their opportunity of being employed.

Reason:
Misconceptions from the employer that they are unable to complete the job when it is actually possible with the additional help of assistive technology.
Employer thinks that giving training and making small accommodations to the blind is too difficult


Accessibility to information

Without vision, the ways of getting information are being limited.
The ways blind people get information are through reading braille books, using braille display (translating words in the computer into braille), and through audio.

*braille = a form of written language for blind people, in which characters are represented by patterns of raised dots that are felt with the fingertips

Braille book Braille display

Overly helpful people

Although helping others is good, but helping others without asking for their permission first might actually cause lots of problems to the others. Sometimes the blind encountered this kind of "overly helpful people" who cut into their ways when they are doing fine and started "guiding" them. This can cause them to be irritated and worse if they reach the wrong place or are being left somewhere they are not familiar with. For example, if the blind people are familier with the place but people insist of helping, the blind might actually need to guide them instead to reach the destination the blind wanted to reach, if not they might reach the wrong location.
