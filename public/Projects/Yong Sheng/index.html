Why I started this page

Where it all started...(prologue 1)

the first time i encountered someone with muscular dystrophy was back in primary school back in Primary 4. Back then my school was collaberating on a Values In Action(VIA) art project with a special needs school. (Rainbow Centre).

Through this collaboration, I was able to learn a lot about muscular dystrophy as my partner had muscular dystrophy.

Prologue 2

Recently as part of my school's VIA in secondary 3 and 4,me and my groupmates wanted to raise awareness about muscular dystrophy seeing that many of my groupmates had heard of it and knew people with this form of disability. However, due to lack of information online and physcially we ended up changing our project.

Resolution

Now given this chance once again, I decided to not let this chance slip by and to raise awareness for a rather uncommon and unknown issue in society.

Besides that, here are more facts about muscular dystrophy.

There are many different types of musuclar dystrophy, being 10 main subdivisions of muscular dystrophy.Here are some facts about these general subdivisions of each form of muscular dystrophy.Mainly summarising the muscles and general area it affects and symptoms. Muscular dystrophy mainly occurs in males more so than females and is inherited.

The Most common type diagnosed in childhood, but still rare being 6 in every 100,000 people
Duchenne Muscular dystrophy

The most prominent type of muscular dystrophy, the most harmful one as well. Symptoms usually are visible at ages 2-3, usually at later stages of their lifes in 20-30's which are rare cases, they require assistance with even their breathing.

Becker muscular dystrophy

Usually confused with duchenne as they have similar symptoms however,people who suffer from becker muscular dystrophy usually do not require as much aid as those with duchenne muscular dystrophy

There are more than 30 different mutations of this disease.
Congenital muscular dystrophy

This form of muscular dystrophy is ussually visable or obvious near birth.Infants who suffer from it usually experience hypotonia or a lack of muscle tone. Also experiencing slower rates of reaching normal motor milestones like walking and crawling.

Learn more
The most common type of muscular dystrophy diagnosed in adults
Myotonic muscular dystrophy

An inherited form of muscular dystrophy, which occurs due to progressive muscle wasting and muscle weakness.There are 2 types of it, in type 1 its' effects are more severe and life expectancy is reduced.In type 2, the effects of it are milder and life expectancy is usually not affected

Learn more
Mainly affects the muscles in the upper-body, most people who suffer from this require a pace-maker to enable their hearts to work normally
Emery-dreifuss muscular dystrophy

Mainly affects the joints,muscles and heart, the joint symptoms(contractures of the elbows, ankles and necks) usually viable during childhood(At around age 10). It is also on the slower side of muscles degenerating and is usually only detectable during their early 20's. Intellectual ability is also not affected.

This is one of the rare diseases where it affects both men and women equally
Limb-gridle muscular dystrophy

Usually affects the muscles closest to the body, the pelvic,thigh,shoulders and upper arms.The fourth most common type of muscular dystrophy.Occuring in 2 in every 100,000 people. It usually affects people whom suffer from it in childhood to be more severe and those diagnosed with it in adulthood are less severe and slow.

Mainly affects the muscles of the face, usually appear before age 20 to 40.
facioscapulohumeral Muscular dystrophy

This form of muscular dystrophy has 2 types, of FHSD type 1 and 2. It also does not shorten life expectancy. Usually the most common sign of this disorder is that people are unable to close their eyes during sleep. Usually around 90% of people who suffer from this experience symptoms before the ages of 20. This disease occurs usually in 4 in every 100,000 people.

This form of muscular dystrophy is the slowest form of muscular dystrophy usually taking only affect from ages of 40-60
distal muscular dystrophy

Usually starts with weakness in arms and legs before gradually spreading to other parts of the body with shrinking muscules.It usuallly begins with the weakening of the hands,feet,lower arms or lower legs. If it is severe the muscles may degenerate until a point where even mobility of the limb is difficult.

Mainly affects the muscles of the eyelids and parts of the throat, the pharyx.It occurs in only 1 in every 100,000 people. Symptoms for this variation usually are only obvious until the 40-50 years old region.
