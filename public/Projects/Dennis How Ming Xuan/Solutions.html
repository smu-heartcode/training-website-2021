INDIVIDUALS




1.Be more cautious of the way you speak.
Do not make insensitive and deragatory comments even though you may mean no harm. An example would be the famous phrase," are you blind?"


2. Be more proactive.
When met with someone who seems to be suffering from visual impairment, ask if they need help and not force one's way through.

BUSINESSES


1. Consider installing a text-to-speech audio device
at corners of the stall that would read out the contents of the products to consumers.

2. At workplaces,
more inclusive and user-friendly appliances could be put in place. For example, sensors for those with visual impairment that will buzz when they are about to knock into an object or brailles that will help them understand in an easier way.

IN TIMES OF DISASTERS


In Singapore, the main form of disaster is flashflood.


1. Sensors
can be put in place that will warn people to not approach the flooded area. This can go a long way to help people with visual impairment.
