WHAT IS VISUAL IMPAIRMENT?

Impaired means weakened or damaged

and

visual impairment would mean that

one's line of sight has been weakened

or damaged, causing them to be unable

to see clearly.

There are mainly three categories of visual impairment.





























background information



"The World Health Organization (the WHO) classifies visual impairment based on two factors: the visual acuity, or the clarity of vision, and the visual fields, which is the area from which you are able to perceive visual information, while your eyes are in a stationary position and you are looking straight at an object.

The Snellen Chart is used to test visual acuity. Your visual acuity is calculated using two numbers. The first number is the distance between the person reading the chart and the chart. The second number is the distance that a person with normal vision would have to stand from an object to see what you did at 20 feet. For example, a visual acuity of 20/80 means that you can read the chart from 20 feet away as well as a person who could read the chart from 80 feet away."

types of visual impairment

low visual acuity

visual acuity between 20/70 and 20/400 with your best corrected vision

blindness

a visual acuity of 20/400 or worse with your best corrected vision

legal blindness

a visual acuity of 20/200 or worse with your best corrected vision
